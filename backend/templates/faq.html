Frequently Asked Questions

The IMEI (International Mobile Equipment Identity) is a unique 15-digit number that identifies your specific mobile device. It's like a fingerprint for your phone. The easiest way to find it is by dialing *#06# on your phone's keypad. You can also find it in your phone's settings (usually under "About Phone" -> "Status") or printed on the original box.

Yes! You can submit a report directly through our "Report Stolen Device" page. You will be required to upload proof of ownership (Receipt/Box) and a valid Government ID. Your report will be listed as "Pending" until verified by our partner police officers, after which it will be officially added to the global blacklist.

To prevent abuse and prank reports, we do not instantly blacklist devices reported by the public. A "Pending" status means your report has been received and is currently being reviewed by a police officer. Once they verify your ID and Receipt, the status will change to "Stolen" and the blacklist will become active.

You strictly need two things:

  1. Proof of Ownership: A photo of the phone's receipt or the original packaging box showing the IMEI number.
  2. Valid Identity: A clear photo of your NIN, Voter's Card, Driver's License, or International Passport.
Reports submitted without these proofs will be rejected by the verifying officer.

Using the online reporting tool on SafeIMEI is free. However, if your case requires a physical investigation or police extract, standard administrative procedures at the police station may apply. SafeIMEI does not charge you for blacklisting a device.

Do not buy it. Buying a stolen phone is a crime (Receipt of Stolen Property) and can lead to arrest. Walk away from the deal. If you feel safe, you can click the "Alert Police" button on the search result page to anonymously tip off the authorities about the location of the device.

Verification times vary depending on the workload of the State Command you selected. Typically, reviews are completed within 24-48 hours. You can check the status of your report at any time using the Reference ID provided to you.

If you find your phone, please visit the police station or contact support with your Reference ID. An officer will verify that the device is in your possession and update the status to "Recovered," removing the public warning from the database.

While it is technically possible for advanced criminals to alter an IMEI, it is a difficult, risky, and illegal process that often permanently damages the phone ("bricking"). Most common thieves do not have the tools or expertise to do this. SafeIMEI makes the resale of stolen phones significantly harder and less profitable, acting as a powerful deterrent against the vast majority of thefts.
